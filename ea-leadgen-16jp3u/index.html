01The thesis

Why the money is in triggers, not in the word "economics".

Nobody wakes up wanting "economic advisory". They wake up to a CMA letter, a contested quantum figure, a price-control deadline, a subsidy challenge, or a board paper that won't survive scrutiny. The S&W pages are written exactly this way — the hub leads with "complex cases when analytical rigour, strategic judgement and credibility count", and every service line opens on a buyer under pressure, not a methodology.

The whole strategy is one loop:

Monitor a regulatory / legal / commercial event → map it to the service page that already sells the answer → publish a short expert insight (development → why it matters → what to do now → how we help) → offer a practical checklist or triage as the lead magnet → route the lead to a named-partner CTA (Schellion Horn for competition/finance, Jonathan Gillham for disputes).

This beats broad "economic advisory" lead-gen because each trigger self-selects a buyer who has budget, a deadline, and a fear of getting it wrong in front of a regulator or court — exactly the high-fee work the practice is built to win.

03Seven trigger-based lead plays

Each maps a live event to a service page, a buyer, and a hook. Ranked by fee potential.

1 · DMCC pricing-journey review High fee

"Are your pricing journeys defensible under DMCC / CMA scrutiny?"

Trigger: The Digital Markets, Competition and Consumers Act 2024 gives the CMA direct consumer-enforcement powers over drip pricing, urgency/scarcity claims, fake discounts and default opt-ins. Buyer: consumer-facing retail, ticketing, travel, marketplaces, SaaS, subscriptions. Routes to: Competition services + Economics of strategy. Owner: Schellion Horn.

2 · Price-control / WACC challenge session High fee

"Is your cost-of-capital case capturing today's political and geopolitical risk?"

Trigger: Live price-control cycles (PR24, RIIO, NR, aviation) and CMA redeterminations; geopolitical risk poorly reflected in allowed returns. Buyer: water, energy, telecoms, transport, airports. Routes to: Regulatory & competition finance + Regulatory economics. Owner: Schellion Horn.

3 · Regulatory efficiency-model stress test High fee

"Do legacy benchmarking models still reflect decarbonisation, climate resilience and cyber risk?"

Trigger: Efficiency/totex benchmarking embedded in price controls increasingly contested at appeal. Buyer: regulated infrastructure companies and regulators. Routes to: Regulatory & competition finance + Valuation & inference. Owner: Schellion Horn.

4 · Subsidy-control defensibility pack Mid–high

"Can your subsidy decision survive CAT / CMA scrutiny with a clear counterfactual?"

Trigger: Subsidy Control Act regime maturing; SAU referrals and CAT challenges rising. Buyer: public authorities, delivery bodies, government-backed projects, their legal advisers. Routes to: Competition services. Owner: Schellion Horn.

5 · High-value dispute quantum review High fee

"Build a defensible counterfactual before pleadings harden."

Trigger: Pre-action / early-stage high-value disputes where quantum and causation are contested. Buyer: law firms, GCs, corporates, investors, insolvency practitioners. Routes to: Disputes & litigation + Valuation & inference. Owner: Jonathan Gillham.

6 · AI / algorithmic-harm economic assessment High fee

"Translate model failure, algorithmic pricing, bias or data misuse into economic impact."

Trigger: First wave of AI-implementation failures, algorithmic-pricing collusion concerns and data-misuse claims. Buyer: digital platforms, AI vendors, legal teams, competition counsel. Routes to: Disputes & litigation + Valuation & inference. Owner: Jonathan Gillham.

7 · Pricing-strategy & revenue-optimisation review Mid–high

"Optimise pricing while staying defensible under competition and consumer scrutiny."

Trigger: Inflation/margin pressure pushing dynamic pricing — straight into DMCC risk. Buyer: CFOs, strategy teams, commercial directors, PE portfolio companies. Routes to: Economics of strategy (bridges to Competition). Owner: Schellion Horn.

06Ten outbound campaign angles

Subject-line + opening-line angles for sequenced outreach. Each leads with the trigger, not the service.

  1. DMCC enforcement is live — "The CMA can now fine for drip pricing directly. Are your checkout journeys evidenced?"
  2. Allowed returns vs. real risk — "Your next price control will be set on a WACC that may not reflect today's geopolitics."
  3. Legacy benchmarks, new world — "Decarbonisation and climate resilience aren't in your efficiency model. The regulator's still using it."
  4. Subsidy under challenge — "If your subsidy decision were referred to the CAT tomorrow, would the counterfactual hold?"
  5. Before pleadings harden — "The cheapest time to fix a quantum case is before the number is in the particulars."
  6. When the model is the defendant — "Algorithmic pricing and AI failure are becoming economic-damages cases. We quantify the harm."
  7. Cross-examination test — "Three questions a barrister will ask your expert — and whether the evidence survives them."
  8. Motor finance / FCA redress — "Sector-wide redress turns on causation and consumer harm. That's an economics question."
  9. Pricing power without the headline risk — "Capture margin with dynamic pricing — without becoming the next DMCC case study."
  10. The business case that won't survive the board — "Independent challenge to your NPV/IRR case before it reaches investment committee."
Sequencing. Pair each angle with the matching §5 lead magnet as the first-touch offer, the relevant insight article as second touch, and a named-partner "start the conversation" as third. Keep volume low and targeted — these are five-to-six-figure engagements, not a newsletter list.

09Appendix — run-ready Firecrawl config & extraction schema

Preserved from the brief so the crawl can be re-run for refresh/expansion when an API key is available. The strategy above was built by reading the pages directly; this is for systematic re-extraction at scale.

Two-step: map, then crawl only accepted URLs

# pip install firecrawl-py pydantic
import os, json
from firecrawl import Firecrawl
from firecrawl.v2.types import ScrapeOptions

START_URL = "https://www.swgroup.com/services/economic-advisory/"
app = Firecrawl(api_key=os.environ["FIRECRAWL_API_KEY"])

crawl = app.crawl(
    START_URL,
    limit=25,
    include_paths=[r"^/services/economic-advisory/.*"],
    scrape_options=ScrapeOptions(
        formats=["markdown"],
        onlyMainContent=True,
        removeBase64Images=True,
        blockAds=True,
    ),
)
print(f"Crawled {crawl.completed} pages · credits {crawl.credits_used}")
with open("sw_economic_advisory_crawl.jsonl", "w", encoding="utf-8") as f:
    for doc in crawl.data:
        f.write(json.dumps({
            "title": getattr(doc.metadata, "title", None),
            "url": getattr(doc.metadata, "url", None) or getattr(doc.metadata, "sourceURL", None),
            "markdown": doc.markdown,
        }, ensure_ascii=False) + "\n")

Why these settings: include_paths keeps the crawl inside the economic-advisory tree; an explicit limit=25 caps cost (Firecrawl's default is high); onlyMainContent + markdown gives clean text for extraction. For a refresh, run /map first to discover URLs, then crawl only the accepted list.
