UK Economics Consultancy: A Market Overview

Four pieces on a £2.28 billion industry built from every Companies House filing for around 90 firms. The market, the ownership, the people, the money.

Published April 2026 · ~50 minutes total reading time

If you want the long-form history, Derek Ridyard's 2002 walkout of NERA, the LECG collapse, Jacobs's £3 billion acquisition of PA, that lives in the 12-part historical series. This shorter set is the snapshot version: what the market looks like today, who owns the firms, who runs them, and where the money actually goes.

Part 1 · 11 min read
The £2 Billion Industry Nobody Talks About
£2.28bn combined revenue across 23 firms that file full statutory accounts. Four firms above £250m account for almost two-thirds of total revenue. Half foreign-owned, half not. The market shape, by the numbers.
Part 2 · 14 min read
Who Actually Owns These Firms?
Three archetypes, independents, subsidiaries, partnerships, plus the holdcos, family trusts, and one McKinsey acqui-hire being wound up on schedule. Eight US parents dominate by revenue.
Part 3 · 12 min read
The Economics Consultancy Network: The Moves That Actually Matter
I went looking for concurrent cross-board directorships and found almost none. What the data shows instead is a market that moves: AlixPartners' build-out, Compass Lexecon to Econic, Grant Thornton's new practice, the CMA-to-consulting pipeline.
Part 4 · 13 min read
The Money: Where Economics Consultancy Actually Prints Cash
Debt facilities, growth trajectories, top-earner pay, and the structural reasons why one firm generates £3.3m per partner while another runs at 1.4% margin.
Method. Built from Companies House filings for around 90 UK economics consultancies, audited against firm websites, GCR, Concurrences, court filings, and contemporaneous press. Every numerical claim reconciles to a source filing; every named person to a primary or secondary record. A reader who spots a factual error is invited to contact the author and a correction will be published promptly.
