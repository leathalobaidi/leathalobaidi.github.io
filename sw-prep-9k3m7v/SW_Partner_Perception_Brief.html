Five lenses — how different audiences perceive them

1 · Peer lens (other senior consultants and economists)

Schellion's GT exit is read as principled — she chose to walk before the Cinven business plan landed. Jonathan's move from HKA is read as strategic — HKA is disputes-quant heartland; he is taking that DNA into a wider economics practice. Together they're read as having made a careful, considered launch on terms they negotiated, not a forced consolidation.

2 · Client lens (general counsel, regulator-facing teams, policy heads)

Schellion is the named expert you go to for competition damages, subsidy work and securities litigation; Jonathan is the named expert for macro-driven matters and infrastructure appraisal. The dual offer means S&W can pitch for matters that previously needed two firms (e.g. a CAT damages case with an embedded macro counterfactual).

3 · Press / industry-trade lens

Coverage of the joint appointment (Yahoo Finance / industry trades) treated it as a serious entry, not a side-bet. Schellion's media platform pre-existed; Jonathan brings policy-quote profile. The pairing means S&W has two voices to deploy on different stories.

4 · Recruiter (Kez) lens — internal to your interview process

Kez explicitly described them as "chalk and cheese". Schellion does the conversational, relationship-led Stage 1; Jonathan does the technical, methodological Stage 3. The pairing is the test — they want hires who can engage credibly with both registers. This is the structural design of the interview, not an accident.

5 · In-team lens (the people they will recruit and lead)

A practice with a media-fluent extrovert rainmaker plus a partner-level practising econometrician is, internally, an attractive proposition: career upside on relationship/business-development and technical-credential paths. Both routes have a named role-model at partner level. This is unusual — most practices have only one or the other.

What this means for your interview run

With Schellion (4 June, Stage 1)

  • Match her conversational energy without competing for airtime — let her talk, take notes by hand.
  • Pattern-match on governance: Wenta NED + SPE Council + Mind Treasurer mirror her trustee/NED portfolio.
  • Lean on your regulator-side origin (Ofgem) — she went from Big Four to NHS to consulting; you went from regulator to BBB to regulated entity. Different shapes of the same "public-to-private-and-back" arc.
  • Show subsidy-control awareness without overclaiming — she co-edits the publication.
  • One concrete reaction to her "Why infrastructure regulators should rethink efficiency modelling" piece. That is your signal that you've read her, properly.

With Jonathan (Stage 3)

  • Expect technical probes. He will not be hostile; he will be precise.
  • Do not bluff quant depth. He is the practising econometrician at partner level. The cost of pretending is high; the cost of saying "I'd want to triangulate that" is zero.
  • Lean on the regulatory finance work that is in your fingers — building blocks, BMA mechanics, variance reconciliation, RY chapters. This is genuinely your home turf.
  • Signal causal-inference taste through Anthropic Fellowship RCT design and BBB Start Up Loans evaluation, without overclaiming BLP-grade structural depth.
  • The Nottingham link is real but light — he did the PhD, you did the MSc. Mention it once in passing if natural, don't lean on it.
